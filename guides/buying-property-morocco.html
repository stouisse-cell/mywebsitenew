General Buyer Guide

Buying Property
in Morocco.

A practical overview for anyone trying to understand the Moroccan purchase process before choosing a property, reserving a unit, or sending money.

Core idea

The property is only one part of the decision.

The better question is whether the price, developer, paperwork, location, timeline, and use case all make sense together.

The Basics

What Buyers Need to Understand First.

Buying in Morocco can be straightforward when the information is organized. The difficulty is that many good projects are not presented with the same level of detail buyers expect abroad.

01

Developer Check

Look beyond the apartment itself. Ask who the real estate developer is, what they have delivered before, and what documentation is available.

02

Price Context

Compare price per square metre, location, finishes, parking, delivery timeline, and resale or rental logic before judging value.

03

Legal Process

A Moroccan notary verifies title, manages the legal process, and registers ownership through the Land Registry.

Before Reserving

Questions Worth Asking.

A reservation should come after enough clarity, not before it. If the answers are vague, slow, or informal, that is information too.

What is the exact surface area and final price?
Is parking included and titled?
What finishes, materials, windows, doors, kitchens, and equipment are included?
What is the delivery timeline and what happens if it changes?
What documents can be reviewed before reservation?
Who is responsible for follow-up after you reserve?

Market Reality

Good Projects Are Not Always Well Presented.

In Morocco’s more established foreign-buyer markets, agencies, listings, and rental managers are easier to find. In secondary cities, especially in the middle market, strong real estate developments may still be sold through direct calls, local contacts, construction-site enquiries, and word of mouth.

01

The Missing Website Problem

Many middle-market real estate developers do not invest in polished websites, brochures, or detailed buyer materials, even when the actual building is strong.

02

The Sales Contact Gap

A sales contact may know the price and surface area, but not the deeper answers on materials, finishes, title, delivery timeline, or real estate developer history.

03

The Buyer’s Job

The buyer needs to organize scattered information into one decision: price, location, quality, paperwork, timeline, use case, and risk.
