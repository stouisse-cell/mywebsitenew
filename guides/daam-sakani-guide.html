Daam Sakani Guide

A Subsidy Is Useful
Only If the Property Makes Sense.

Daam Sakani can help qualifying buyers access ownership, but the property still needs to be judged on location, quality, price, and long-term value.

Practical point

Daam should not mean settling for less.

The strongest opportunity is when support helps a buyer access a better asset than they thought was possible.

Programme Basics

What Daam Sakani Can Do.

The Daam guide describes state support for qualifying Moroccan first-time buyers. The programme can provide 70,000 MAD for homes priced between 300,001 and 700,000 MAD, subject to eligibility and rules.

01

First-Time Buyers

Daam is designed to help buyers access ownership, especially households that are currently renting or priced out of stronger locations.

02

MRE Eligibility

The MRE guide notes that MRE buyers can be eligible, and that a meaningful share of beneficiaries are Moroccan residents abroad.

03

Programme Window

The guide material frames Daam Sakani as available until 2028, making timing part of the decision.

Before Applying

The Subsidy Is Not the Whole Decision.

A buyer still needs to understand the property, the obligations, and the long-term use case.

Confirm the exact unit price and whether it qualifies.
Understand primary residence obligations and restrictions.
Confirm whether the buyer has owned property before.
Review financing, notary costs, and remaining cash required.
Evaluate location, build quality, future resale, and rental logic.

Buyer Fit

Daam Is a Tool, Not the Whole Strategy.

The subsidy can improve affordability, but the buyer still needs to choose a property that makes sense. A weak location or poor-quality building does not become a good decision because support is available.

Compare the property to non-Daam alternatives in the same city.
Check whether the building quality supports long-term value.
Understand the primary residence commitment and future rental limitations.
Make sure the monthly payment still fits the household budget after fees and setup costs.
