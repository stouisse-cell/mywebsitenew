The Basics

Foreign Buyers Can Own
Property in Morocco.

Morocco allows foreign nationals to purchase residential property in their own name. Some buyers are planning a move, some want to rent first and understand the market, and others are looking for a property that can produce rental income or long-term capital growth. In every case, the details still matter: bank setup, notary process, title verification, transaction costs, rental rules, and how money moves in and out of the country.

✓

Own in Your Name

Foreign buyers can purchase residential property under their own name in Morocco's urban areas, with notarised ownership and Land Registry registration.

✓

No Residency Required

You do not need to live in Morocco to buy. With proper representation, parts of the process can be handled remotely through power of attorney.

✓

Money Movement Matters

A convertible dirham account is an important step for foreign buyers who want a clear route for qualifying rental income and future sale proceeds.

✓

Renting or Earning Income

Foreign clients may choose to rent before buying, occupy the property themselves, or rent it on a short-term or long-term basis after purchase, with income declared under the applicable Moroccan tax rules.

€

Know the Full Cost

Purchase costs include notary fees, registration, and taxes. We help clarify the full cost before a buyer commits.

%

Financing Can Be Explored

Some Moroccan banks may finance non-resident buyers. We help prepare the right questions and coordinate introductions where appropriate.
