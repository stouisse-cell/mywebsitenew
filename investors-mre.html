Buying a second residence or investment property in Morocco often starts with family advice, local contacts, and a short summer visit. That can help, but it is not the same as structured research, real estate developer checks, location comparison, pricing context, and proper follow-up.

Short Visits, Big Decisions

Too Many Decisions
Are Made With Too Little Detail.

Many MRE buyers only have a few weeks in Morocco. In that time, they may hear about two or three developments through family, pass a building under construction, ask around, and end up speaking to a sales contact who cannot answer the questions that matter.

This becomes especially risky with pre-construction. Buyers may be shown a blueprint once, asked to reserve quickly, and expected to leave a down payment before they have clear information on the architect, materials, finishes, real estate developer history, delivery timeline, payment schedule, or what happens if the project changes after they return abroad.

Diaspora buyers are also vulnerable to paying more than the local market supports.

01

Without clear comparable pricing, an MRE buyer may not know whether the price offered is fair for the neighbourhood, finish level, floor, view, parking, and delivery timeline.

02

Some sellers may change terms or increase the price when they sense the buyer has limited time, limited local information, or strong emotional motivation to buy back home.

03

We help compare projects, check pricing logic, ask for missing details, and keep the discussion grounded before a reservation or down payment is made.

Pre-Construction

The Best Price Is Often
Before the Building Is Finished.

For many MRE buyers, pre-construction is where the best opportunity sits. Prices are often strongest before completion, unit choice is wider, and payment schedules can be more flexible. The problem is timing. You usually need to be present, informed, and ready during a narrow window, and most diaspora buyers do not have months in Morocco to compare projects, visit sites, negotiate terms, and follow up.

That is where mistakes happen. Some buyers leave a substantial deposit after an informal reservation, return to Europe or North America for a year, and come back to delays, changed specifications, unanswered calls, or, in the worst cases, a project that was never properly secured. Pre-construction can be a strong strategy, but only when the real estate developer, paperwork, payment schedule, and construction progress are properly monitored.

How we support pre-construction buyers

We help review the real estate developer, project status, payment terms, reservation process, promised finishes, expected delivery, and construction timeline. Once work is underway, we can provide regular site follow-up and weekly construction updates so you are not left guessing from abroad.

Daam Sakani

MRE Buyers May Qualify
for State Housing Support.

Daam Sakani is Morocco's direct housing support programme for eligible buyers, including Moroccans living abroad. Depending on the buyer and the property, support can reach up to 100,000 MAD, making it an important factor for MRE families buying a qualifying primary residence.

Eligibility is not automatic. It can depend on nationality, whether you already own residential property in Morocco, whether you previously benefited from state housing aid, the property price, first-sale status, completion documents, and the residence-use rules attached to the programme.

What we help verify

We help you identify whether a project is worth checking for Daam Sakani eligibility, what questions to ask the notary, what documents matter, and when the timing of a pre-construction purchase may affect the process. Final eligibility must always be confirmed through the official platform and notarial process.
